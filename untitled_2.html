Paths Yet Untraveled: Things That I Could Be



Sometimes I wonder what would’ve happened if I took writing seriously.

Back in high school, I used to jot down random thoughts in notebooks, on the back of receipts, even on my phone at 2 AM when I couldn’t sleep. Short dialogues, unfinished poems, scenes that played out in my head like clips from a movie. A few teachers told me I had potential. One even said, “You could be a writer.” I didn’t think much of it at the time, but that comment stayed with me longer than I expected.

There was something about writing that felt... right. Natural. Like it let me slow down and actually process the world. But as I got older, life started demanding more practical things. I leaned into tech. It felt safer, more structured—coding, design, web development. Skills that paid bills. Skills people actually hired for.

And honestly, I don’t regret it. I’ve learned a lot, worked with some great people, and I like building things that actually function. Still, every now and then, I catch myself staring at a blank Google Doc or scribbling in the margins of a notebook again. There’s this voice in the back of my mind that says, “You could’ve written that story. You could’ve finished that novel idea you had back in 2020.”

I keep a folder on my laptop named “Someday.” It’s filled with unfinished drafts, random titles, half-baked characters. I open it once in a while just to remind myself that the writer in me is still there—quiet, maybe, but not gone.

I didn’t choose writing. Not fully, anyway. But part of me still hopes that one day, even if it’s just for me, I’ll write the kind of story I always wanted to.

Maybe I’m not a writer by profession. But I’m still someone with something to say.

And maybe that’s enough for now.




Written by Nikko — a techie with a writer’s heart.
