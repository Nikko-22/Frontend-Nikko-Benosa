Maybe I Should've Chosen Psychology

An honest thought on feeling lost in a competitive world — 4 min read

I’ve been thinking about it more lately —

Maybe I don’t belong here.

Not in a dramatic, throwing-my-laptop-out-the-window way. Just… quietly. Like I’m walking through a room full of people who all seem to know what they're doing, while I’m still trying to remember why I walked in.

Some days I open up my laptop, stare at the code editor, and feel like I'm faking everything. Tutorials pile up. New tools pop up every week. There’s always someone younger, faster, smarter.

And I can’t help but wonder — what if I had taken that Psychology course when I had the chance?

I remember back then, I was torn. I liked how the mind worked, how people behave, how emotions shaped decisions. But tech felt like the right move. The one with better opportunities, better pay, more future. Everyone said so.

So I went for it. I learned Front end Development. I told myself, “You got this.” And for a while, I did. I made a few things. Some looked decent. I was proud.

But now? The industry feels… heavy. Like I’m running to catch a train that keeps getting faster. Like I blinked and suddenly I’m behind again.

And here’s the thing:

It’s not that I hate tech. I actually still like building stuff. But I also wonder what my life would look like now if I had leaned into understanding people instead of pixels. If I had followed that small voice instead of the loud advice.

I guess what I’m really saying is:
I feel lost.
And I don’t know if I’m just burned out, or if I’m truly in the wrong place.

But maybe that’s okay. Maybe it’s part of figuring things out. Maybe a lot of people feel this way but never say it out loud.

So here I am — not quitting, not giving up, just pausing.
Sitting with the question:
“Do I really want to be here?”
And waiting to hear whatever honest answer comes next.
