Maybe It’s Not Supposed to Happen Right Away

— A story about timing, patience, and finding your pace   -- 3 min read --

When I graduated, I thought things would fall into place right away. That all the late nights, requirements, and pressure would finally pay off. That there’d be a job waiting. A path cleared. A “welcome to your dream career” moment.

But it didn’t happen like that.

I watched some of my batchmates land jobs at big companies just weeks after we got our diplomas. I saw Instagram posts of “First day at work!” and LinkedIn updates that sounded like success stories already written.
And I started to wonder — Did I miss something?
Was I already falling behind?

But slowly — quietly — I realized something that brought me peace: We don’t all arrive at the same time.

Some people find their dream job early, and that’s beautiful. But for others, it takes a little longer. Sometimes you need to explore, fail, start over, get lost, or take a detour you didn’t expect — not because you’re doing it wrong, but because your journey isn’t supposed to look like anyone else’s.

I used to think success was a straight line. Now I know it’s more like a messy map — some scribbles, some backtracks, a few blank pages. And that’s okay.

Maybe this phase — this space between where I am and where I want to be — is still part of the story.
Maybe I’m not late.
Maybe I’m just becoming.
